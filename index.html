Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ)

Welcome to the Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ). This questionnaire will help you understand your personal values and how they influence your behavior and decisions.

The PVQ consists of 40 questions that measure 4 basic value categories:

Self-transcendence

  • Benevolence: Preserving and enhancing the welfare of those with whom one is in frequent personal contact (the 'in-group')
  • Universalism: Understanding, appreciation, tolerance, and protection for the welfare of all people and for nature

Conservation

  • Security: Safety, harmony, and stability of society, of relationships, and of self
  • Conformity: Restraint of actions, inclinations, and impulses likely to upset or harm others and violate social expectations or norms
  • Tradition: Respect, commitment, and acceptance of the customs and ideas that one's culture or religion provides

Self-enhancement

  • Achievement: Personal success through demonstrating competence according to social standards
  • Power: Social status and prestige, control or dominance over people and resources

Openness to change

  • Self-Direction: Independent thought and action, choosing, creating, exploring
  • Stimulation: Excitement, novelty, and challenge in life
  • Hedonism: Pleasure or sensual gratification for oneself

For each question, indicate how much the person described is like you.

Conformity

Adhering to social norms and expectations by exercising self-restraint. Encourages politeness, obedience, and responsibility to avoid conflict or disapproval.

Self-transcendence

Benevolence
